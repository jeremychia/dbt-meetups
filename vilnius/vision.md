# Vilnius dbt Meetup: Vision & Growth Strategy

## Where the Meetup Is Now

Vilnius dbt Meetup (branded as "Baltic dbt Meetup") is nascent — exactly 2 events over 5 months (Oct 2024, Mar 2025), both hosted by dbt Labs with local speakers:

**Event #1 (Oct 2024): Inaugural — Foundations**
- Speakers from TeraSky, Oxylabs, Surfshark
- Topics: dbt Cloud intro, dbt Core + Airflow architecture, organizational adoption of analytics engineering
- Format: 3 talks, 2-hour event, ~30-min talks, networking
- Venue: Cyber City, Vilnius (tech hub)

**Event #2 (Mar 2025): Early Growth — Rigor**
- Speakers from TeraSky, Vinted, Kilo Health
- Topics: dbt CI/CD with Argo Workflows, data ingestion (pre-dbt), Airflow + dbt orchestration
- Format: 3 talks, 3-hour event, hosted at Vinted HQ (local company, strong signal)
- Registration via Eventbrite with "limited spots" (attendance capped/curated)

**Pattern**: Introductory-to-intermediate talks grounded in real local companies (Vinted, Surfshark, Kilo Health, TeraSky). Early indicators of healthy adoption by regional companies.

## The Gap & Unique Role

**What Vilnius Dbt Meetup Can Own**

1. **Regional Hub for Analytics Engineering Discipline (Baltics-wide)**
   - Data Science Vilnius is dormant; no other active AE/data infrastructure meetup in Lithuania or Baltics
   - Opportunity to be the first/only practitioner venue for analytics engineering in the region
   - Can attract practitioners from Riga, Tallinn (if format allows), not just Vilnius

2. **Bridge Between Founders/Scale-Ups and Data Practice**
   - Vilnius has 615 startups, many not yet mature enough to have data teams — but growing into the need
   - The meetup can speak directly to founders and early-stage data leaders ("this is how you build durable data platforms as you scale")
   - 2 events showed Vinted, Surfshark, Kilo Health as speakers — these are exactly the companies in the growth-stage window who benefit from peer learning

3. **European Visibility (via Big Data Conference Europe 2026)**
   - Big Data Conference Europe is being held in Vilnius Nov 24–27, 2026, with 700+ attendees and 60+ talks
   - Vilnius dbt Meetup can position as the *local, ongoing* complement to that conference — a way to stay connected year-round
   - Opportunity to host a joint session or "dbt track" conversation at the conference or partner with speakers

## Vision Statement

**Vilnius dbt Meetup is the region's hub for building durable, trustworthy data platforms — meeting the ~50+ companies in the Baltics who are adopting analytics engineering for the first time, and the practitioners leading that charge.**

The meetup's differentiator from Berlin and other mature markets is **"we are building the culture of analytics engineering *here, now, from the ground up.*" This is not a "here's what we've learned" retreat for experts; it's a "here's how we're going to build this together" initiative for a region in transition.**

## The Hybrid Growth Strategy (Year 1–2: Reach; Year 3+: Depth)

### Phase 1 (Now — 2026): Establish & Reach

**Goal**: Build awareness of dbt + analytics engineering as a standard practice in the Baltics. Grow the core group from 2 events to a regular cadence. Attract speakers and attendees from across the region.

**Concrete Actions**:

1. **Set a regular cadence: Every 6–8 weeks** (vs. ad-hoc now)
   - Manageable for organizers; enough frequency to build habit for attendees
   - 6 events/year = credible signal of commitment

2. **Expand geographically: Alternate venues or invite Riga/Tallinn speakers**
   - Host some events in Vilnius (dbt Labs will support), some in Riga or as virtual hybrid
   - Framing: "Baltic dbt Meetup" already implies multi-country scope — lean into it
   - Reach Riga's tech community (Latvian startups, Airbnb-era companies) early, before they build competing groups

3. **Recruit founders & early-stage data leaders as speakers**
   - Not just established companies like Vinted/Surfshark, but also Series A/B startups at the inflection point
   - "How we built our first dbt project" talks resonate with founders building teams
   - Example: Hostinger, Nord Security, other 100–500-person growth-stage companies

4. **Connect to Big Data Conference Europe 2026**
   - Pitch to conference organizers: host a "Vilnius dbt Meetup" session or track during the conference (Nov 24–27)
   - Drive conference attendees to the regular meetup: "Start here with the local community; dive deeper at the conference"
   - Creates natural funnel: local meetup → big conference → back to local community for ongoing learning

5. **Codify the "newcomer-friendly" positioning**
   - Event intros: "If your company is building a data team, or if you're the first dbt/AE hire, this is for you"
   - Curate talks that speak to scaling (Vinted's ingestion story, TeraSky's CI/CD), not just advanced optimization
   - Include 1–2 "primer" talks per 4–5 events explaining dbt fundamentals, analytics engineering career paths

### Phase 2 (2027+): Deepen & Sustain

**Goal**: Build a durable core of Baltics practitioners who keep showing up. Shift from growth to community depth.

**Concrete Actions**:

1. **Introduce continuity mechanisms** (same as Berlin model)
   - "Revisit" slot: Follow up on topics from 6–12 months ago; track what actually shipped
   - Public open-questions backlog in Slack channel
   - Recurring "teach back" sessions where attendees from past meetups host small deep-dives

2. **Regional practitioner community** (not just a meetup)
   - Slack channel for Baltics data/AE professionals (#baltic-dbt or similar)
   - Job board for local AE/data roles (leveraging growing demand as market matures)
   - Mentor matching: pair junior AEs with more experienced practitioners

3. **Host a "summit" or conference session**
   - By 2027, the region will have matured enough to host its own regional dbt/AE event
   - 1–2 day summit with workshops, talks, panels from local practitioners
   - Positions Vilnius as the regional hub (not just a meetup, but the authority venue)

## Format & Logistics (Next 6 Events, Through End of 2026)

**Cadence**: Every 6–8 weeks (roughly monthly-ish cadence, flexible for holidays/holidays/conference periods)

**Format** (proven by Events #1 & #2):
- 2.5–3 hour events (registration to close of networking)
- 3–4 talks of 25–30 min each (including Q&A)
- 1 opening slot for dbt Labs updates + other tools/sponsors (optional, rotating)
- Built-in networking time (30–45 min pre-talks, 30 min post-talks)
- Food/drinks provided (Vinted HQ model worked well)

**Venue Strategy**:
- Alternate between Vilnius tech hubs (Cyber City, Vinted HQ, other scale-ups) to showcase multiple companies' offices
- Q4 2026: Consider a Riga satellite event or virtual hybrid to test regional reach
- Long-term: Rotate venues to Riga, Tallinn annually (once infrastructure exists)

**Recruitment**:
- Target speakers from companies at 50–500 headcount inflection point (building their first AE team or scaling)
- Invite 1–2 "primer" talks per event (newcomer-focused; dbt fundamentals, career paths, "what is analytics engineering anyway")
- Leverage local dbt Labs connection for occasional speaker support (but keep it peer-led; avoid over-dependence on dbt Labs talks)

**Marketing**:
- Meetup.com (existing group; already at meetup.com/vilnius-dbt-meetup)
- Luma events posting
- Slack #local-baltic channel in dbt Community Slack
- Direct outreach to tech communities (Lithuanian tech association, startup incubators, university computer science programs)
