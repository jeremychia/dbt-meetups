# Berlin dbt Meetup: Co-organiser Sourcing Profile

## Purpose
Reference criteria for screening and selecting 2 rotational co-organiser leads for the Berlin dbt Meetup. Use this as a filtering lens over existing candidates to prioritize alignment with the operational model (Hub-and-Spoke, low-overhead async coordination) and the community's technical/demographic composition.

---

## Growth Archetype (Priority)
**Look for:** Sharp data professionals actively seeking to build personal brand and visibility in Berlin's data community, even if they have zero prior event-running experience.

**Why this matters:** The framework (operational playbook, support crew, quarterly rotations) de-risks inexperience. We're investing in *growth-minded people*, not event-production veterans. Someone on their way up will own the role with more energy than a senior delegate.

**Red flag:** Passive, "happy to help if you need me" energy. Pass. We need initiators.

---

## Technical Balance Target
**Gap to close:** Currently, Berlin event speakers/content leans heavily toward BigQuery and Redshift/DuckDB local dev stacks. **Across 14 past events, no talk or speaker bio mentioned Snowflake, Databricks, BigQuery, or Iceberg / open table formats.**

**Sourcing priority:** Recruit co-organisers (or coach them to recruit speakers) from Snowflake / Databricks / Iceberg user communities. This is a genuine gap, not an assumption.

**Why:** Brings a different technical perspective to the community, expands the talent pool we expose to, and signals that Berlin's data community isn't siloed into a single stack.

---

## Ecosystem & Demographic Mix

### Scale-ups vs. Enterprise Balance
Current candidate pool **already has strong scale-up representation** (Qonto, Urban Sports Club, Forto Logistics, Formel Skin, ex-Blinkist, Enpal GmbH) and solid enterprise/traditional tech presence (T-Systems, Rib Software, Solution Xpert).

**Action:** Use this as a lens to confirm 2-3 selections avoid clustering all in scale-ups or all in enterprise. Aim for one of each, if possible.

### Female Data Leadership
The outgoing organisers (Eva Schreyer, Victoria Perez Mola, and others) represent strong female voices in the community. The *new* cohort should maintain or strengthen this.

**Action:** Actively prioritize female data professionals among the final 2-3 selections. Frame this as continuity of leadership voice, not tokenism.

### German vs. Expat / International Balance
Berlin has a strong expat tech community alongside German nationals. Look for candidates who represent both, to reflect the actual makeup of the city and avoid an insular feel.

**Action:** Lightweight check during screening: "How long have you been in Berlin? Does that shape how you see the data community here?" This isn't a hard requirement, but a signal for balance.

---

## Content Philosophy: "Scars over Features"

**Standard:** Coach all speakers (and co-organisers, in their intro speeches/selection of themes) toward raw engineering failures, data contracts, migration friction, and team dynamics — not vendor announcements or "5 ways to optimize your dbt runs" listicles.

**Exemplars from past events:**
- **Noel Spencer (Enpal, Sept 2024):** "Tougher Than Berghain Bouncers: Crafting a CI Pipeline for Your dbt Repo That Turns Away the Unworthy" — concrete guardrails, not theory.
- **Tim Hiebenthal & Victoria Perez Mola (Dec 2024):** "Making the most out of dbt tests" and "If your code gets an attitude, test it like it's hot!" — candid, battle-tested takes on testing.
- **Marielle Dado & Eva Schreyer (Aug 2023):** "How to Win Friends and Influence People When Migrating to dbt" — organizational friction, change management, not technical specs.

**Action:** Share these examples in the co-organiser onboarding. When recruiting speakers, ask: "What's one thing that surprised or frustrated you about this project?" and lean into the answer.

## Optional Screening Discovery Prompts

Use these in your 1:1 candidate calls if they feel natural. Adapt, skip, or swap as you prefer — these are tools, not a script.

### Growth Check
*"Are you actively looking to build your profile in the Berlin data community? How does owning a high-visibility project for one quarter support your career growth right now?"*

Listen for: Clarity about personal goals (brand, network, skill-building), not vague "sounds fun" energy.

### Vision Check
*"If you had total creative freedom for one night of meetup content, what advanced engineering problem would you focus on, and whose office or venue would be the right fit for that conversation?"*

Listen for: Specificity, ownership, perspective. Shows they've thought about what Berlin's community *needs*, not just what existing formats offer.

### Async Check
*"We coordinate quarterly planning via a shared Markdown/Notion document and async decision-making to protect everyone's volunteer time. We aim for zero status meetings. Are you comfortable driving results and owning a whole quarter's lineup with that constraint?"*

Listen for: Comfort with async work, ability to follow a written plan and raise async questions/problems, not dependency on real-time meetings.

## Practical Use
1. **Before your screening calls:** Print or bookmark this doc. Glance at the Growth Archetype and Content Philosophy sections to calibrate your ear.
2. **During screens:** Use the discovery prompts if they land, but don't force them.
3. **After screening (narrowing round):** Re-read Ecosystem & Demographic Mix and Technical Balance sections. Do your top 2-3 together give good balance? Or are they too similar?
4. **In the selection call with Jeremy:** Reference the Content Philosophy and Growth Archetype as a shared language for why you're excited about a candidate.

## Notes
- This profile is *not* a final checklist. No single candidate will check every box. Use it as a lens, not a scorecard.
- If you find someone who scores high on Growth Archetype but is unfamiliar with Berlin's event scene, that's okay — the Hub-and-Spoke framework and Jeremy's support net that risk.
- The "Scars over Features" bar is something *you* model in conversations with candidates and speakers, not something you quiz them on.
